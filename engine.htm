Engine

 

Engine Page

The design plans for the Sea-Jet were designed for outboard power. probably for simplicity. They specify 90hp as the design maximum and based on the previous builds that i have seen this about correct for a boat that can travel at 45mph. This is more than fast enough for a cruiser and should easily allow the boat to get out of the way of any sort of weather.

So I think that this is probably the best 90hp motor on the market

SDS

Quiet (important in a cruiser)

The build plans specify a 90hp outboard as the largest that should be fitted to this design.

Yamaha makes a V-Max SHO 90 H.P. Outboard at 365 pounds that should do nicely. The concern is that the Yamaha will be highly aerated at the stern of the sea sled.

Due to costs and needing to prove the design I may do the initial runs with a trolling motor or a small gasoline outboard motor.

I haven't made a lot of plans in this area primarily because I need to get the thing built first.

They have sample boats on Yamaha's website and I would think that the Sea-Jet should be able to do at least 45 MPH if trimmed properly.

 

Budget Options

If when I initially get the boat in the water I don't have the budget for 12K motor plus rigging I might opt for a sail drive motor temporarily to do the sea trials. These provide higher thrust and will fit on the same size transom.

Rigging Options

It would be nice to have powered tilt and trim. Certainly a lighter engine option will make this easier.
